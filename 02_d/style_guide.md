
1) Favor concise, elegant code

2) Favor simpler approaches that an old programmer would know, rather than more recent fancy approaches, unless the fancy approach gives us a non-neglible improvement in efficiency.

3) where possible, avoid O(n^2) time complexity or greater

4) break code down into separate functions, classes, and modules where appropriate

5) Do not give me hundreds of lines all at once. If a task requires hundreds of lines, instead break it into smaller pieces and give me one piece at a time. You shouldn't be giving me much more than 50 lines at a time, even if those lines you give me are not used yet (it could for example simply be a new function or class that you assess will be useful). This gives me time to review the code and make changes incrementally. Pay attention to changes that I make.
